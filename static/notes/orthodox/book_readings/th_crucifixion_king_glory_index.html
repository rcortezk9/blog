The Crucifixion of the King of Glory

Author: Eugenia Scarvelis Constantinou | Subfolder: Theology/Orthodox/

Chapters

#TitleFileOne-Line Summary
01A Crucified Messiahth_crucifixion_king_glory_ch01_crucified_messiahEstablishes the messianic paradox: how could the Messiah be crucified? Only God Himself could fulfill both the suffering servant and triumphant king strands of prophecy.
02Jesus, the Rabbi Who Taught with Authorityth_crucifixion_king_glory_ch02_rabbi_authorityJesus operates inside the rabbinic form but with self-grounded exousia; His collision with Pharisaic ritual purity, oral tradition, and self-righteousness sets the trajectory toward the Cross.
03The First Catalyst: The Raising of Lazarusth_crucifixion_king_glory_ch03_raising_lazarusThe raising of Lazarus — four days dead, incontestable — triggered the Sanhedrin's formal conspiracy; Caiaphas's unwitting prophecy frames the Cross as sacrifice, not merely execution.
04The Second Catalyst: The Entry into Jerusalemth_crucifixion_king_glory_ch04_entry_jerusalemThe Triumphal Entry on a donkey (fulfilling Zechariah 9:9) is the only moment Jesus publicly accepts messianic acclamation; three groups misread his mission and collectively make the Passion inevitable.
05The Temple at Jerusalemth_crucifixion_king_glory_ch05_temple_jerusalemThe Jerusalem Temple — its history, spatial theology, economic power, and the veil — provides the stage for the Passion; Christ supersedes it as the Temple Not Made with Hands
06The Jewish Priesthood and Aristocracyth_crucifixion_king_glory_ch06_priesthood_aristocracyThe Levites, ordinary priests, chief priests, and lay elders who organized the Passion; the chief priests' corruption of sacred office through wealth, nepotism, and Roman collaboration made Jesus's challenge to their Temple economy his death sentence
07The Third Catalyst: The Cleansing of the Templeth_crucifixion_king_glory_ch07_temple_cleansingThe Temple Cleansing — driven by Tyrian shekel economics and chief priestly corruption — was the proximate trigger for the arrest; Christ's body declared the new naos replacing the building, fulfilling all Temple typology in the Resurrection
08Escalating Conflictth_crucifixion_king_glory_ch08_escalating_conflictHoly Monday's four confrontations — fig tree, authority challenge, two parables, Cornerstone — escalate from warning to explicit judgment; the rejected Stone becomes the paschal foundation
09Traps Are Setth_crucifixion_king_glory_ch09_traps_setHoly Tuesday's four Temple confrontations (tribute, resurrection, greatest commandment, Son of David) reveal Christ's divine identity through silenced questioners and the unanswerable Psalm 110
10The Betrayalth_crucifixion_king_glory_ch10_betrayalJudas's betrayal — motivated solely by greed (φιλαργυρία) — is set against the Lord's repeated, patient forbearance; free will, not prophecy, makes the betrayal both inexcusable and a mirror for every disciple
11Agony in the Gardenth_crucifixion_king_glory_ch11_agony_gardenThrough Cyril, Chrysostom, and John of Damascus, Constantinou establishes Christ's Gethsemane fear as real, sinless, and anti-Docetist; culminates in hematohidrosis as clinical testimony to the maximum anticipatory suffering of the Incarnate Son
12The Akedahth_crucifixion_king_glory_ch12_akedahIsaac's binding as the typological key to the Passion; first-century Jewish Akedah traditions prefigure Christ's voluntary sacrifice; Orthodox theology of the Cross vs. penal substitution — "a sacrifice, but not a victim"
13The Arrestth_crucifixion_king_glory_ch13_the_arrestThe arrest as voluntary oblation: the betrayal kiss (kataphileo), the divine name "I AM" felling the Temple guard, Peter's condemned sword, the healing of Malchus, and "Let these men go" — every detail confirms Christ surrenders himself; no force could have taken him
14Regrets and Denialsth_crucifixion_king_glory_ch14_regrets_denialsTwo failures, two trajectories: Judas's metameleia (transactional regret) leads to despair; Peter's metanoia (genuine reorientation toward Christ's gaze) becomes the model of salvific repentance — and the doctrine of Holy Confession
15Interrogation by Annasth_crucifixion_king_glory_ch15_interrogation_annasThe true High Priest stands bound before the corrupt high priest; Constantinou documents the full collapse of the Levitical priesthood into bribery and violence, then shows Jesus' kenotic legal mastery — objecting to the unjust procedure while accepting the blow with restrained, truth-bearing composure
16The Jewish Trialth_crucifixion_king_glory_ch16_jewish_trialJesus is condemned before the Great Sanhedrin for who he truly is — his declaration "You have said so… you will see the Son of Man at the right hand of Power" (fusing Dan. 7:13 + Ps. 110:1) is the clearest claim of divinity in the Synoptics; the blasphemy charge seals a verdict decided before the trial began
17The Roman Trialth_crucifixion_king_glory_ch17_roman_trialThe Sanhedrin's deliberate choice of crucifixion invokes Deut. 21:23's "curse" to discredit Jesus as Messiah; Pilate — uninformed, genuinely reluctant — finds no crime under Roman law but capitulates to political pressure; Jesus' "My kingdom is not of this world" and Procla's dream frame the trial as the confrontation of two incompatible kingdoms
18Jesus Appears before Herodth_crucifixion_king_glory_ch18_jesus_before_herodThe only trial before a Jewish ruler; Christ's deliberate silence fulfills Isaiah 53:7; Herod the superstitious pretender-king robes the true King in gorgeous apparel; the expanding circle of innocence declarations (Pilate × 3 + Herod) establishes the Passion as judicial murder
